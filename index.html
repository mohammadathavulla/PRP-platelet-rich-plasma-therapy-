About PRP


It seems there might be a small typo in your question; you're likely referring to "platelet-rich plasma therapy" (PRP therapy). Platelet-rich plasma is a treatment that involves drawing a small amount of the patient's blood, processing it to concentrate the platelets, and then injecting the PRP into the injured or affected area.

Platelets are blood cells that play a crucial role in the body's natural healing process. They contain growth factors and other proteins that contribute to tissue repair and regeneration. PRP therapy is used in various medical fields, including orthopedics, sports medicine, dermatology, and more.

OverVIew of PRP

Here's a general overview of the PRP therapy process:

1.Blood Collection: A small amount of the patient's blood (usually a few ounces) is drawn.

2.Centrifugation: The blood is placed in a centrifuge, a machine that spins rapidly to separate its components. This process isolates the platelets from other blood components.

3.Platelet-Rich Plasma Extraction: The concentrated platelets are then extracted to create platelet-rich plasma.

4.Injection: The PRP is injected into the targeted area, such as joints, tendons, or the scalp, depending on the purpose of the treatment.
